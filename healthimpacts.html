The Effects of the Digital Divide on Healthcare

The digital divide affects more than internet access. It also affects how people find doctors, schedule appointments, use telehealth, learn about treatment options, and access health insurance information.

Why does this matter?

When people do not have reliable internet or the skills to use it, they may struggle to get timely healthcare. This can lead to delayed treatment, worse health outcomes, and deeper inequality between communities.

Limited Healthcare Access

People in low-income, elderly, and rural communities may have fewer ways to reach healthcare providers online.

Difficulty Finding Affordable Care

Without strong internet access, people may miss lower-cost healthcare options, programs, or nearby services.

Lack of Digital Skills

Some people may not know how to use healthcare portals, telehealth platforms, or online insurance tools.

Delayed Treatment

When access to care is already limited, lack of internet can make health problems worse over time.

Wider Health Disparities

The digital divide increases the gap between people who can easily access care and those who cannot.

Rural Appointment Barriers

In some rural areas, both physical and mental health appointments may already be limited, and poor internet makes access even harder.
