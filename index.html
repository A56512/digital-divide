Bridging the Digital Divide for Better Health and Wellness

What is the Digital Divide?

The digital divide is the gap between people who have access to technology and those who do not. This gap affects education, healthcare, and everyday opportunities.

Over 14 million people in the U.S. lack reliable internet access.

As more services move online, people without reliable internet or digital skills are left behind. This creates inequality in access to essential services like healthcare.

Why It Matters

Limited Healthcare Access

People may struggle to find doctors, clinics, and healthcare services without internet access.

Mental Health Support Gaps

Online mental health resources are harder to access for those without reliable technology.

Lack of Health Information

Some individuals cannot easily find accurate health information when they need it most.

Higher Costs

Without access to online tools, people may miss affordable healthcare options.
